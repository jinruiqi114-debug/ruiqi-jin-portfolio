Visual culture and brand strategy. Between Paris and Shanghai.

I am Ruiqi Jin (靳睿琦). I study how images carry power — how high fashion organises looking, how brands build cultural authority, and how tradition gets remade in the present. I work as a researcher, curator and brand strategist, and I model on the side to keep one hand on the object of study. 我是靳睿琦。我研究图像如何承载权力——高级时装如何组织观看，品牌如何建构文化权威，传统如何在当代获得新生。我的身份横跨研究者、策展人与品牌策略师，同时也以模特身份保持对研究对象的直接感知。

§ 01 — About§ 01 — 关于我

In the gap
between art
and commerce.连接艺术与商业的学术背景。

Paris · 2026

From the Central Academy of Fine Arts to Paris-Kedge Business School, I trained simultaneously in art-historical method and in management. As a model I lived the discipline of being looked at; as a curator I learned to organise the looking; as a strategist I use both views to help brands build cultural authority. 从中央美术学院到法国 Paris-Kedge 商学院，我同时接受艺术史方法论与管理学训练。作为模特，我经历了"被看"的规训；作为策展人，我学会了组织"观看"；作为策略师，我将两种视角结合，帮助品牌建构文化权威。

The questions I keep returning to: how does the visual transmit power? How do brands construct cultural belonging through space and the body? How do traditional symbols survive — and become new — in the present? 我反复思考的问题：视觉如何传递权力？品牌如何通过空间与身体建构文化归属感？传统符号如何在当代存活，并生成新的意义？

看与被看，并非对立关系。
它们是同一种权力关系的两面。
由制度决定，而非个人选择。 To see and to be seen are not opposites.
They are two faces of the same power relation.
Determined by institution, not personal choice.

My practice and research focus on the construction of visual mechanisms and cultural authority. 目前的实践和研究聚焦于视觉机制和文化权威的构建。

Case · 02 · Heritage案例 · 02 · 非遗传承

雕经镂纬
典籍里的缂丝Carved Warp
Etched Weft · Kesi

A two-track project on the silk-tapestry craft of kèsī: tracing its vocabulary, techniques and surviving works through historical texts, while rebuilding the loom, the gesture and the looking inside an exhibition hall. 一个关于缂丝工艺的双线项目：在历史文献中追溯其语汇、技法与存世作品，同时在展厅内重建织机、动作与观看体验。

Role角色
策展 · 研究 · 文字撰述Curation · Research · Writing
Venue地点
苏州 · 丝绸博物馆临展Suzhou · Silk Museum temporary exhibition
Output输出物
展览 + 中法双语画册 + 文创原型Exhibition + bilingual catalogue + cultural-creative prototype
Period时间
2020 — 2024
Curatorial note策展手记

缂丝不是"丝绸"。
它是一种以"通经断纬"为言说方式的画。 Kèsī is not "silk." It is a painting spoken through the grammar of continuous warp and discontinuous weft.

If kèsī was the most prestigious of Chinese silk crafts, why does it now survive in the hands of a few aging masters in remote towns? We decided to re-introduce them through the books — from Catalogue of Imperial Kesi Calligraphy and Painting to the Chinese-French Chinese Silk Literati Painting — so that text and machine could meet in the same room.

The title wall lifted four characters: 《雕经镂纬》 — shuttles falling from a black overhead bay, threads pulled into a rising blue diagonal. Our abstract translation of the two gestures of the craft, and the visitor's first sentence after entering.

A working loom sat in the centre of the gallery for live demonstrations; on the other side, a wall of antique paintings with woven cushions invited visitors to sit and look at the centuries. The bilingual catalogue, commissioned for the opening, became the show's second output.

媒体之夜
Pathless WoodsMedia Night
Pathless Woods

A media night at Longfor's Pathless Woods hill-house. We re-wrote a press visit as a night in the mountains — three photographable scenes, no scripted line, an official line that came out of the writers' own pens.

Client客户

Brief概述

把"媒体参访"重新书写成"山里的夜晚"。让媒体老师主动为品牌写东西。 Re-write the press visit as a night in the mountains. Make journalists choose to write about the brand.

Results成果

Media attended媒体到场

Articles published发稿量

Total reach传播曝光

下午以《悠然·见山前》主题旗帜入场，天幕与老树划出重点区；黄昏是天幕下酒会与二十分钟的思考题；入夜后是山谷里的串灯、独坐、与那些不干补的对谈。 The afternoon began with 《悠然·见山前》 banners marking the camp; dusk brought a tent-side aperitif and a twenty-minute open prompt; after dark, valley lights, solitude, and conversations no one planned.

现场被拆成四个可拍摄场景，每个场景都有一份"写什么都可以"的提示词，也有一份"不要写"的黑名单。这是把论文里的"观看距离"思路带到现场——不安排"被看"，反而让品牌被"看见"。 The event was structured as four photographable scenes, each with an open prompt and a blacklist. A direct application of the thesis concept of "viewing distance" — instead of staging the brand to be looked at, we let it be seen.

输出不仅是 5.2M+ 的传播与 42 篇发稿，更是一本《山前 · 星天外》现场眼本——它后来变成了该项目后续萤门拉客的官方语言。 The output wasn't only 5.2M+ reach and 42 articles — it was a field journal, 《山前 · 星天外》, that later became the official language for the venue's ongoing guest programme.

§ 03.2 — Core findings§ 03.2 — 核心发现

Looking, as
a system of
power.观看，
作为一种
权力系统。

The four propositions the thesis returns to. Each is testable; each has practical implications for how brands organise their image.论文反复回归的四个命题，每一条都可验证，每一条都对品牌形象的建构有直接的实践启示。

1

时装秀是视觉权力的装置，而非单纯的服装展示Fashion shows are an apparatus of visual power, not merely a display of clothing.

2

品牌通过空间设计、身体编排、灯光控制来组织"观看"，从而定义"合法的品味"。Brands organise the gaze through spatial design, choreography, and lighting control, thereby defining "legitimate taste."

3

模特的身体经历"去主体化"过程，成为品牌的视觉媒介。Models undergo a process of "de-subjectification," their bodies becoming the brand's visual medium.

4

不同品牌使用不同的视觉策略来构建文化权威，这些策略反映了对"距离"的不同管理方式。Different brands employ different visual strategies to construct cultural authority, each reflecting a distinct approach to managing "distance."

§ 04 — Runway§ 04 — 秀场

Seen and to be seen.看，与被看。

Modelling is where I learned what I now study. Standing in the looked-at position, you feel exactly how the body is disciplined, how the gaze is organised, how the brand turns presence into meaning. This section is half portfolio, half ethnography. 模特经历是我理解所研究课题的起点。站在被看的位置，你切身感受到身体如何被规训、凝视如何被组织、品牌如何将在场转化为意义。这一部分，一半是作品集，一半是田野笔记。

Polo Ralph Lauren · Paris · 2026

The static show
as visual discipline.静态展示
作为视觉规训。

Not a runway. A static exhibition, where models hold position for 3–4 hours while VIPs come close to look, touch the fabric, and photograph. 这不是走秀，而是静态展览。模特保持姿态长达 3–4 小时，VIP 宾客近距离观察、触摸面料、拍摄留影。

"你不是快速走过然后离开，而是持续暴露在凝视之下。在那个空间里，我不是一个人，而是品牌的一个视觉元素。" "You don't walk past and leave. You remain continuously exposed to the gaze. In that space, I wasn't a person — I was a visual element of the brand."
